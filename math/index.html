Math and Programming Advice

I highly encourage anyone interested to start training for math contests! (but don’t force yourself to if you hate it). I personally felt that math kept me upwind and also happened to align with my previous interests.

The most helpful advice post I’ve received was math contest platitudes. In particular, premature optimization is the root of all evil was the most important thing I took away! It’s also mentioned in Atomic Habits: there’s a difference between action and motion; and the best way to start improving is to take action.

What’s been mentioned can apply to any skill, including competitive programming. Competitive programming and math competitions are quite related in terms of staying upwind, problem-solving skills, and building intuition.

Resources I’ve used:

  • Math:
    • MAST (discontinued)
    • OTIS
    • AMC Past Problems
    • User created mock contests
  • Competitive Programming:
    • Codeforces
    • USACO Past Problems
    • Competitive Programmer’s Handbook
    • CSES Problemset
    • USACO Guide
