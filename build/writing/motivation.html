Motivation

For anyone who has had to self-study competition math or something similar in nature, they may have heard the word motivation, probably mentioned so that one can actually use it to study harder.

There’s also motivation in solving math problems, but that’s a different kind that is based off of experience and intuition, and what the problem “feels like” it should be. Rather, the motivation I’m talking about is the drive to actually work.

Intrinsic vs Extrinsic Motivation

There are a lot of ways that motivation could be coming from. Here are a few examples:

  • Fear from something external
  • Reward from something external
  • Competitive nature
  • Enjoyment

I think a lot of times, the reasons why people work are for the external factors, which is extrinsic motivation. This means doing things so your parents get mad, so your teachers like you better, but most common these days, so it looks good to colleges. This is pretty bad, because then you are just working so you can get the result; if the result never appears, then one might feel that it was for nothing.

Intrinsic motivation is usually when someone does the work for themselves. In this scenario, they have to actually enjoy the work, and often find it easier to work harder; the results just come naturally, you don’t need to chase the results.

I would personally say that what type of motivation you need really depends on who you are and what you are doing. Things like athletics and academic competitions should have more of a intrinsic motivation, while doing well in school is more extrinsic motivation.

Power of Motivation

In a math contest perspective, intrinsic motivation is best for training and can really get one to spend hours at a time studying and still enjoy it. If someone is doing math competitions and don’t have this motivation, then they should probably reconsider why they are doing it (because in math contesnts, just like real life, results don’t always come from hard work).

However, it’s fine if you can’t have the focus or can’t see why for now; maybe a little push from your parents can help, even if it feels like a bit of interference. From personal experience, singing me up for classes that are slightly above my difficulty level help, because then I have to do the homework and the homework is challenging enough for me to spend a considerable amount of time.

No matter what, keep in mind the type of motivation that you have for what you are doing, and use it well.
