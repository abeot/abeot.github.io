Productivity

I think a common misconception that people give you is that the more effort you put into something, the better you will be at it. This is pretty blatantly wrong, and I can speak from personal experience; I often find my classmates spending plenty more hours studying for a test, when I only need to review my notes for five minutes. In short, I think it’s more important to study smart, not hard.

Studying

Whenever you study, the goal shouldn’t be to spend as much time as possible, it should be to learn as much as possible or get the most done in a fixed amount of time. This is especially true when studying for a test; you shouldn’t be redoing every assignment or reading the whole chapter; instead, just capture the most important pieces of information that was summarized.

A lot of learning actually happens in class though, so it’s more important to pay attention then. In fact, classes are so repetitive that just from hearing the teacher say it enough you will probably remember.

Time

At this point, you probably have been able to minimze the time that you spend on your actual schoolwork. When it comes to doing homework or any sort of menial work, what I do for myself is set a time limit and get the most done during that time, or set a goal and get it done as fast as possible. The motivation is often to have time to study for math or coding, and this is much more efficient because it forces you to stop wasting time (given that you have a motivation to get it done, which you should).

It also helps to be trying to do work whenever it’s possible; personally for me, two of my school classes have pretty lazy teachers so I can usually get a bit of homework done, so I don’t need to do it at home. Some people may not have this luxury, but paying attention is still extremely important.

Tools

I know some people also use other tools or tricks to help. Personally, I think distractions like your phone shouldn’t even matter if you have a purpose when you are working, but if you just can’t help yourself, put it away and don’t think about it. I’ve also found that trying to work in a different room so you develop the habit of being efficient in that new place helps, but it doesn’t really matter since at the end of the day being efficient is something internal.

As for productivity apps, I use Todoist as an app to write down any assignments/work I need to do, it’s really easy and fast. I previously used Notion but it’s a little harder in terms of ease of use; however, I like how easy it is to quickly type up schedules and organize projects, but in general I don’t use it that much. Mainly these apps just boost my productivity because I know exactly what to do, which saves time from thinking about what to do. It’s the same thing with being productive, usually a good first step is to just start getting things done, and stop the premature optimization.
