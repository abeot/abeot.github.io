title: Math and Programming Advice 
desc: Some advice for others.
---

I highly encourage anyone interested
to start training for math contests! 
(but don't force yourself to if you hate it). 
I personally felt that math kept me 
[upwind](https://paulgraham.com/hs.html)
and also happened to align with my previous interests.

The most helpful advice post I've received was
[math contest platitudes](https://blog.evanchen.cc/2019/01/31/math-contest-platitudes-v3/).
In particular, *premature optimization is the root of all evil*
was the most important thing I took away! It's also mentioned
in *Atomic Habits*: there's a difference between action
and motion; and the best way to start improving is to take *action*.

What's been mentioned can apply to any skill, including competitive
programming. Competitive programming and math competitions are quite
related in terms of staying upwind, problem-solving skills, 
and building intuition.

Resources I've used:

- Math:
    - MAST (discontinued)
    - [OTIS](https://web.evanchen.cc/otis.html)
    - [AMC Past Problems](https://artofproblemsolving.com/community/c3510133_maa_amc)
    - [User created mock contests](https://artofproblemsolving.com/community/c594864_aops_mock_contests)
- Competitive Programming:
    - [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com/)
    - [USACO Past Problems](https://usaco.org/index.php?page=training)
    - [Competitive Programmer's Handbook](https://cses.fi/book/book.pdf)
    - [CSES Problemset](https://cses.fi/problemset/)
    - [USACO Guide](https://usaco.guide/)

If you want some specific resources best suited for you, 
please feel free to contact me!
